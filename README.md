I build AI-agent systems, MCP servers, automation infrastructure, and the production machinery that keeps "AI magic" from turning into an incident report with a timestamp.

Senior architect. Hands-on builder. I perform controlled demolitions on elegant nonsense.

I care about systems that survive contact with users, bureaucracy, bad networks, legacy code, public institutions, Monday mornings, and other natural disasters.

> **Deterministic checks are law. LLM judgment is counsel. Humans are sovereign.**

That principle shapes how I design AI infrastructure: agents should be useful, observable, auditable, and powerful without being granted the authority of a caffeinated god process.

---

## What I build

I design infrastructure where LLMs are not treated as divine oracles, but as useful, dangerous, and slightly overconfident junior coworkers with tool access.

My current work sits at the intersection of:

- AI-agent systems and MCP ecosystems
- Gov-tech and public-procurement data
- Search, analytics, and SEO automation
- Open-source infrastructure
- Production web platforms
- Developer tooling for people who actually ship

"AI-powered" is not an architecture. It is a warning label unless proven otherwise.

## Operating philosophy

- Build for production, not demos.
- Prefer boring reliability over theatrical cleverness.
- Keep humans in authority loops.
- Make agents useful before making them autonomous.
- Design control surfaces, not black boxes.
- Logs are evidence. Dashboards are witnesses. Vibes are inadmissible.
- Treat AI as leverage, not absolution.
- Never confuse model confidence with system correctness.
